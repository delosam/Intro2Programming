Brief Introduction to The World Wide Web

The World Wide Web in it's abbreviated form is WWW. It was created and introduced in the early 1990's. The World Wide Web is comprised of about 30 billion pages that are made up of the following:

  • A collection of HTML documents
  • HyperText Markup Language
  • Hyperlinks

What Are the File Types Hosted on the Web?

There are quite a few file types that can be found on the Internet. Among them are:

  • Plain Text
  • HTML
  • Images
  • Video
  • Music

What Are the Components to The Web?

The web is made of the following objects:

  • A living person
  • A Computer
  • A Browser to make call outs to pages
  • The Internet
  • Servers that hosts files

So how does an individual call out to a page like Udacity for example to see what they have to offer? The individual will open his browser of choice. I personally prefer Google Chrome, Internet Explorer has seen better days,in my opinion. The individual will type the web address in the address bar and hit enter on the keyboard. The browser will search the Internet, and eventually locate a server hosting the specific file that was requested by the individual. This is probably a good segue for the next section - discussing web pages and what they are made of up of.

What Are Webpages?

Webpages are files that are made of up HTML. HTML is short for hypertext markup language, and is apparently fairly easy to use according to the pros or in this case, Scott our trainer in the IPND. Webpages consist of the following:

  • Text content (what you see)
  • Markup (What it looks like in the browser window)
  • Refereces to other documents (images and videos)
  • Links (to other pages)
